What To Expect After Applying

  • Universities will send SMS or email confirmations.
  • Most will give you a student number to track your application.
  • Start preparing certified documents for uploads (ID, results, affidavits, etc.).
  • Set reminders for follow-up dates, deadlines, and outstanding tasks.
  • Universities might take weeks or months to respond—be patient but alert.

Universities That CHARGE an Application Fee

These usually range from R100 to R300 and must be paid during or after applying:

  • University of Pretoria (UP)
  • University of Cape Town (UCT)
  • University of the Free State (UFS)
  • Stellenbosch University
  • Rhodes University

Universities That WAIVE Application Fees

  • University of Johannesburg (UJ)
  • University of South Africa (UNISA)
  • University of Limpopo
  • University of Venda
  • Mangosuthu University of Technology

Universities Likely With Fees (Not Confirmed)

  • University of KwaZulu-Natal (UKZN)
  • North-West University (NWU)
  • Nelson Mandela University
  • Cape Peninsula University of Technology

Universities Likely With Fees (No Specific Waivers Found)

Recommendations

  • Set calendar reminders to track responses and deadlines.
  • Download NSFAS forms if applying for financial aid: nsfas.org.za
  • Keep checking your email and SMS inboxes.
  • Join university WhatsApp groups or Facebook groups to stay updated.

Final Checklist: What To Do After Applying

  • Have digital & certified copies of your ID, results, affidavits, etc.
  • Log in weekly to university portals using your student number.
  • Ensure your contact details are correct in the application profile.
  • Start preparing backup options in case your application is rejected.
  • Use NSFAS, bursaries, or learnership platforms to prepare funding.

⚠️ Important Notice

While we do our best to keep this information up to date, university application policies and fees may change at any time. We highly recommend double-checking directly with the official university websites or contacting their admissions offices to confirm the latest requirements, deadlines, and fee structures.
